my book recs

The Code Breaker

Walter Isaacson

A gripping account of how Nobel Prize winner Jennifer Doudna and her colleagues launched a revolution with CRISPR. It dives deep into the ethics of gene editing, the competitive nature of modern science, and the race to develop the technology that will define the future of medicine. Essential reading for anyone interested in the complexities of bioethics and the human side of scientific discovery.

Bioengineering History

I Contain Multitudes

Ed Yong

This book completely changed how I view the microscopic world. Yong takes us on a tour of our own bodies, revealing them not as individuals but as vast ecosystems. It challenges the binary view of microbes as solely pathogens, illustrating the intricate, symbiotic relationships that sustain life. It provides a beautiful ecological framework for understanding the human microbiome.

Biology Ecology

Biodesign: The Nature of the Future

William Myers

A visually stunning survey of the intersection between biology and design. This book showcases projects that use living materials—from furniture grown from fungi to clothes made of algae. It pushes the boundaries of what we consider "materials" and proposes a future where we grow our products rather than manufacture them. A huge inspiration for my own work in synthetic biology interfaces.
