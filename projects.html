Research Projects

Personalized Diabetic Wound Healing: A CRISPR Based Synthetic Biology Approach Using Engineered Probiotics

Tools: Python, PhysiCell, Benchling, NetLogo

Designed and computationally validated a "smart bacteria" system using engineered probiotics to sense a diabetic wound's microenvironment and deliver therapeutic proteins (DNase I, IL-10) via CRISPR activation.

  • Key Achievement: ISEF Finalist (2025)
  • Key Achievement: 1st Place Winner, Washington State Science & Engineering Fair (2025)

Identifying Possible Biological Processes Affected By Non-Target Proteins of SARS-CoV-2

Tools: NCBI BLAST, AlphaFold 2, RPKM data analysis

Investigated potential non-ACE2 viral targets to understand mechanisms of Long Covid, identifying the protein Collectrin as a key area of interest.

  • Key Achievement: Selected as an AJAS/AAAS Fellow and presented this research at the national AAAS conference (2024)

Mitochondrial Mutational Pathways in Neurodegenerative Diseases (Review Article)

Status: Ongoing

Authored a comprehensive research review article proposing a new framework for understanding diseases like Parkinson's and Alzheimer's based on specific mitochondrial mutational pathways.
