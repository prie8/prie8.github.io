My Origin Story: Science as Personal Action

Originally presented as my 2-minute speech at ISEF 2025.

Good morning, everyone. My project, "Personalized Diabetic Wound Healing: A CRISPR Based Synthetic Biology Approach Using Engineered Probiotics," isn't just a scientific endeavor for me; it's deeply personal.

Growing up here in America, some of my most cherished memories are the visits from my grandparents from India. But alongside the joy, I also witnessed something that profoundly affected me: their daily struggle with diabetes. I saw firsthand how this disease impacted their lives, especially my grandfather. Simple things became difficult, and their quality of life was often diminished. One of the most challenging aspects for him, and for us as a family watching, was the development of diabetic foot ulcers – wounds that just wouldn't heal, constantly at risk of infection, and causing persistent pain. It was heartbreaking to see them suffer, knowing that millions worldwide face this same debilitating complication.

That experience, watching the people I love grapple with the severe consequences of diabetes, became a driving force behind my research. I wanted to find a better way, a smarter way, to help.

The problem with diabetic wounds is often the stubborn microbial biofilms that form, shielding harmful bacteria from antibiotics and preventing healing. My project proposes a novel solution: an engineered probiotic system, a kind of 'living medicine,' designed to intelligently combat these biofilms.

This research is more than just code and simulations for me. It's a way to channel my love for my grandparents and my desire to alleviate the suffering I witnessed into tangible scientific action. It's about harnessing cutting-edge tools like CRISPR not just for academic exploration, but to create solutions that could one day improve the quality of life for people like them.
